# Social Studies of Science — Author Guidelines

## About the Journal

Social Studies of Science is an international peer-reviewed journal that publishes
research on the social dimensions of science and technology. The journal has a
strong tradition in science and technology studies (STS), encouraging
interdisciplinary work that bridges sociology, history, philosophy, and policy
studies of science and technology.

Published by SAGE Publications.
ISSN: 0306-3127 (Print), 1460-3659 (Online)

## Aims and Scope

Social Studies of Science welcomes submissions that analyze the social processes
involved in the production, dissemination, and use of scientific and technological
knowledge. The journal publishes empirical and theoretical articles that contribute
to the interdisciplinary field of science and technology studies (STS).

Topics of particular interest include:
- Laboratory studies and ethnographies of scientific practice
- The social construction of scientific knowledge
- Science and technology policy
- Public engagement with science
- The sociology of scientific institutions
- Science, technology and inequality
- Digital technologies and society
- AI, algorithms and social life
- Environmental science and politics

## Article Types

- **Research Article** (8000–12000 words): Original research with empirical or
  theoretical contribution
- **Review Article** (6000–10000 words): Critical survey of a research area
- **Commentary** (3000–5000 words): Response to published articles or emerging
  debates

## Submission Requirements

### Manuscript Format
- Double-spaced, 12pt font
- Title page with author details (separate from manuscript)
- Abstract: 150–200 words
- Keywords: 4–6
- Word count includes references and notes
- British or American English (consistent throughout)

### References
- Harvard (author-date) citation style
- Recent references expected (substantial engagement with literature from last 5 years)
- Typically 40–80 references for research articles
- Self-citation should not exceed 10% of total references

### Figures and Tables
- High-resolution figures (300 DPI minimum)
- Tables in editable format
- All figures and tables referenced in text

### Ethics and Declarations
- Data availability statement required
- Conflict of interest statement required
- Ethics approval statement required for research involving human participants
- Funding acknowledgment required
- Author contribution statement (CRediT) encouraged

### AI Disclosure
- Authors must disclose any use of AI tools in manuscript preparation
- AI tools cannot be listed as authors

### Review Process
- Double-blind peer review
- Typical timeline: 8–12 weeks for first decision
- Desk rejection rate approximately 50%

### Open Access
- Hybrid journal (traditional and OA options)
- APC for gold OA: approximately £2,500 / $3,200

### Indexing
- Scopus
- Web of Science (SSCI)
- DOAJ (for OA articles)
