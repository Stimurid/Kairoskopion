## Aims and Scope

Open platform for academic communication between philosophers, science studies scholars, and researchers from neighboring fields. Key objectives:

- Provide space for open scientific communication in philosophy and science studies
- Increase visibility of STS (Science, Technology, and Society) studies in Russian scholarship — described as "an area still insufficiently explored and non-institutionalized in the Russian research and education space"
- Analyze the role of new information and digital technologies in transforming science, education, culture, and society
- Study scientific activity within laboratory settings (STS research tradition)

**Subject areas:** ontology, epistemology, history of philosophy, history/methodology/philosophy of science, logic, STS, philosophical cognitive studies, ethics, aesthetics, philosophy of religion, philosophy of law, philosophy of culture.

**Special emphasis** on "issues related to societal, cultural, and knowledge transformation influenced by digital and ICT technologies."

## Submission Requirements

- **Article length:** 4,000–7,000 words; reviews: 3,000–5,000 words
- **Languages:** Russian or English
- **Format:** Times Roman 14pt, 1.5 line spacing, 2cm margins, 1.25cm first-line indent, justified, no auto-hyphenation, no page numbers
- **Abstract:** 200–300 words (max 350); must explain research field, objectives, relevance, proposed solution, methodology
- **Keywords:** up to 10
- **Submission:** two separate files emailed to editor@digital-scholar.unn.ru: (1) file with author info + title + abstract + keywords; (2) file with title + text only (anonymized for blind review)
- **Citation style:** in-text `[Author, year: page]` (e.g., `[Geach, 1967]`, `[Pross, 2015, p. 28]`)
- **References:** Russian sources require transliteration (translit.net) plus English translation
- **Original, unpublished material only.** No simultaneous submission
- **Author guidelines URL:** https://digital-scholar.unn.ru/en/submit-paper/

- **Evidence status:** FACT_FROM_OFFICIAL_SOURCE (digital-scholar.unn.ru/en/submit-paper/)

## Peer Review

- **Model:** Double-blind peer review
- All submitted articles undergo double-blind review for originality, ethics, and significance
- Authors receive initial screening notification within ~3 weeks
- Editor-in-Chief and deputies select two qualified reviewers (from editorial board or external)
- Review timeline: ~2 months (3 months for foreign-language articles)
- Decision notification: within 2 weeks of screening
- COPE-compliant (Committee on Publication Ethics)
- Rejected manuscripts not returned; reviews retained 5 years
- Confidentiality enforced for both authors and reviewers
